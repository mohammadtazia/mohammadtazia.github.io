So What is the Assembly?

The Assembly is a legislative body consisting of students selected from all active chapters—each chapter is allowed two Assembly seats, whose members are decided by their chapter presidents. The Assembly’s main purpose is to draft and debate on planks that reflect JSA’s stances on current events.

All About Planks

Planks are forms of written legislation that focus on current issues or sentiments that Assembly members wish to address. They are very similar to the bills passed during Winter Congress, and are intended to represent the stances of JSA as an organization.
